ABOUT FORMULA STUDENT

Formula Student is an international engineering competition where university teams design, build, and race a formula-style car. Artificial Intelligence (AI) plays an increasingly significant role, especially in autonomous vehicle challenges. Teams implement AI algorithms for self-driving capabilities, using technologies like computer vision, path planning, and sensor fusion. AI enables the car to recognize track boundaries, avoid obstacles, and optimize racing strategies. Machine learning models process data from cameras, LiDAR, and GPS to make split-second decisions. AI in Formula Student promotes innovation in autonomous systems, aligning with industry trends in automated mobility. Students gain hands-on experience with cutting-edge AI tools, enhancing both technical and teamwork skills. The competition encourages sustainability, efficiency, and pushing the boundaries of automotive engineering. Formula Student AI showcases the future of autonomous racing, blending engineering excellence with artificial intelligence.

DYNAMIC EVENTS

In Formula Student UK AI (FSUK-AI), Dynamic Events test the performance, reliability, and intelligence of autonomous vehicles built by student teams. These events evaluate how well the car's AI systems, such as perception, path planning, and control, function under realistic racing conditions. The primary dynamic events include:

  • Autonomous Acceleration: The car must drive in a straight line as quickly as possible from a standing start, showcasing its ability to manage speed and control efficiently
  • Skid Pad: The vehicle navigates a figure-eight pattern to evaluate its cornering ability, traction control, and precise navigation
  • Track Drive: The car autonomously completes a series of laps on a predefined track with sharp turns, straights, and obstacles, testing the AI’s path planning and obstacle avoidance
  • Autocross: Similar to Track Drive, this event focuses on completing a single timed lap to demonstrate the car’s agility and accuracy. Dynamic events challenge teams to create AI systems that combine speed, precision, and safety, driving innovation in autonomous vehicle technologies

STATIC EVENTS

In Formula Student UK AI (FSUK-AI), Static Events evaluate the teams' understanding and application of AI in both virtual and real-world contexts. These events test the theoretical and practical foundation of the team's autonomous systems. The key static events are:

  • Real-World AI: Teams analyze and present the current state of Level 4 autonomous vehicles globally. This includes discussing real-world advancements, challenges, and limitations in areas such as sensor technology, AI algorithms, regulations, and public adoption. Teams must demonstrate their awareness of how their own vehicle design aligns with or advances the global progress of autonomous driving technology
  • Simulation development: Teams showcase the use of simulation environments to design, train, and test their AI models. They explain how simulations were used to optimize performance, debug algorithms, and predict car behavior before physical implementation. This event highlights proficiency in tools like ROS, MATLAB, or Gazebo, and how simulations reduce development risks
  • Engineering design: Teams discuss the overall architecture of the car, including software, hardware, and system integration. Judges evaluate the engineering principles, creativity, and efficiency of the vehicle's design.
  • Business plan: Teams pitch their autonomous system's potential in real-world applications, beyond just racing. This involves identifying industry use cases, market impact, and the scalability of their AI solutions.
